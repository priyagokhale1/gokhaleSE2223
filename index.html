The Effects of Varying Concentrations of Turmeric on Escherichia coli Growth (2021 - 2022)

Background

The objective of this study was to assess the effects of different concentrations of turmeric on the growth of E. coli. It was hypothesized that the higher the concentration of turmeric in a solution is, the less E. coli would grow. To test this, E. coli were plated on agar and turmeric petri dishes at concentrations of 1 gram, 2 grams, and 5 grams of turmeric powder mixed into the agar. These plates resulted in the least growth of E. coli, especially around the edges of the dishes. Additionally, the absorbency of turmeric solutions were tested with and without E. coli. Data analysis of the plates indicated less of a difference in the absorbency of a higher concentration of turmeric. This supported the hypothesis and showed that E. coli grew the least in the highest concentration of turmeric and grew the most in the lowest concentration of turmeric. As such, it was concluded that turmeric can be an effective treatment against E. coli.

Rationale

The food industry is confronted with several risks regarding the possibility of E. coli contamination—including human infection and foods and water contamination—which need constant prevention and monitoring (Kyriakides). E. coli is one of the most serious food threats faced in both fresh and processed food, with E. coli outbreaks continually increasing especially in raw meats, even after the US Department of Agriculture declared E. coli variants as an adulterant in raw ground beef and required raw ground beef to be tested for the presence of E. coli (McClure). Despite close monitoring, E. coli cannot fully be eradicated, leading to research into incorporating antimicrobial agents into food processing and production to curb E. coli growth.

Research Practicum

This project was done for the Research Practicum course at HTHS. This course requires students to select their own unique research topic, conduct experiments, interpret their results and draw conclusions, all with the help of a research mentor. They are also required to write a comprehensive research paper. This course spans freshman year (ITR) and the first three marking periods of sophomore year. From this course, students learn oral skills, research skills, lab safety, and equips them with the knowledge to conduct their own research as they progress through their careers.
