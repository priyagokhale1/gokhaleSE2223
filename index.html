The Effects of Varying Concentrations of Turmeric on Escherichia coli Growth (2021 - 2022)

Background

Certain strains of Escherichia coli (E. coli) cause foodborne diseases that are known to contaminate leafy vegetables, meats, and fruit. This has caused several E. coli outbreaks in the food industry and consequently put thousands of consumers at risk. Turmeric is a plant known to have natural, antimicrobial agents. The objective of this study was to assess the effects of different concentrations of turmeric on the growth of E. coli. It was hypothesized that the higher the concentration of turmeric in a solution is, the less E. coli would grow. To test this, E. coli were plated on agar and turmeric petri dishes at concentrations of 1 gram, 2 grams, and 5 grams of turmeric powder mixed into the agar. These plates resulted in the least growth of E. coli, especially around the edges of the dishes. Additionally, the absorbency of turmeric solutions were tested with and without E. coli. Data analysis of the plates indicated less of a difference in the absorbency of a higher concentration of turmeric. This supported the hypothesis and showed that E. coli grew the least in the highest concentration of turmeric and grew the most in the lowest concentration of turmeric. As such, it was concluded that turmeric can be an effective treatment against E. coli.
